## Also Notable

### U.S. Government Grants Anthropic Clearance to Release Claude Mythos 5 to Select Organizations

**Anthropic news statement · June 26, 2026 · [anthropic.com/news](https://www.anthropic.com/news)**

The U.S. Commerce Department lifted its June 12 export-control directive restricting access to Claude Mythos 5, allowing approximately 100 vetted U.S. companies and federal agencies to regain access to the model. The original restriction had been imposed on national security grounds, citing the model's unprecedented demonstrated capability to autonomously discover and exploit software vulnerabilities in critical infrastructure without human guidance. Anthropic stated it was restoring access immediately for approved organizations and continuing to work with the government on a broader rollout of both Mythos 5 and Fable 5. This item is a policy/news announcement rather than a research paper, but it directly reflects the state-of-the-art on AI-empowered vulnerability exploitation and the regulatory response it is now prompting.

**Methodology:** N/A — government policy announcement / company statement.

**Why it matters:** The export-control trigger was specifically the model's autonomous cyberoffense capability — this is the first instance of a U.S. government restricting a frontier AI model on security-capability grounds, setting a direct precedent for how AI security research will be regulated going forward.
